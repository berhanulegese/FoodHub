Conclusion and Recommendations¶

Question 17: What are your conclusions from the analysis? What recommendations would you like to share to help improve the business? (You can use cuisine type and feedback ratings to drive your business recommendations.) [6 marks]¶

Conclusions:¶

  1. Delivery Time Plays a Role in Customer Satisfaction

    • Lower-rated orders (e.g., 3 stars) took longer to deliver.
    • Weekends (22 minutes) saw shorter delivery times than weekdays (28 minutes).

  2. Type of Cuisine Influences Delivery Experience

    • Mexican, Spanish, and Vietnamese cuisine saw more variation in preparation and delivery time.
  • Korean and Vietnamese took shorter, consistent times—testifying to smooth operations.

  1. Higher Cost Orders Come with Better Ratings

    • 5-star rated orders had higher mean cost (~$17), suggesting better perception of value.
    • Lower-cost orders got more diverse feedback.

  2. No Good Correlation Between Variables

  • Cost, preparation time, and delivery time are almost uncorrelated (close to zero correlation measures), and thus must be controlled independently.

  1. 11% of Orders Take Over 60 Minutes

    • These are sources of potential dissatisfaction and must be flagged or examined.

  2. Few Restaurants Are Promotable

    • Not many restaurants met on both the high-rating and high-volume bases for being eligible for promotion offers.

Recommendations¶

  1. Enhance Weekday Delivery Times

    • Reduce the ~28-minute weekday mean by:
      • Enhancing route planning
      • Encourage order fulfillment off-peak hours
      • Additional weekday delivery staff

  2. Promote High-Rated, High-Volume Restaurants

    • Promote only restaurants with:
      • Rating count > 50
      • Average rating > 4.0

  3. Operational Improvements Based on Cuisine

    • Focus on cuisines with inconsistent service (Spanish, Mexican) by:
      • Streamlining kitchen operations
      • Staff training to ensure consistency

  4. Encourage Higher-Value Orders

    • Offer bundles or upsells to drive order value, which is positively correlated with higher ratings.

  5. Investigate Extended Delivery Times

  • Identify and resolve issues for orders taking longer than 60 minutes.
  • Explore issuing compensations to affected clients or rescheduling vendor SLAs.
  1. Boost Customer Rating Involvement
  • Leverage post-delivery reminders or offer incentives (e.g., loyalty points) to encourage more users to rate.
